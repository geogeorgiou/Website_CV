Thesis Abstract

A common topic which concerns the Color Industry is the way to optimally reproduce color stimuli identical to the color we are capable of seeing. With Spectroscopic technology we can measure Spectral Reflectances of Colorimetric Charts which are golden standards in Color Science. Multiple light sources are acquired to form a Spectral Power Distribution Database that could help estimate the Spectral Signature of different illumination case scenarios by exploiting Spectral Information of both VIS and NIR Spectrums. Using Spectral Comparison Metrics, Illuminant Estimation is achieved and Color Difference measurements across patches on Spectrally Reconstructed Images will lead to the creation of true, according to human vision, computational color with illumination independence.
